About Claude Monet.

"Claude Monet was born in France in 1840 and passed away in 1926. He is a pioneer of the Impressionist movement, which marked a departure from traditional art forms by capturing nature and everyday life based directly on the artists' observations, particularly focusing on the subtle shifts in light and color. Monet's work demonstrates his skill in capturing moments using light to create atmospheric and sensory experiences. He primarily painted natural landscapes, showing a deep fascination with water reflections, and the changing skies and clouds. Monet often painted the same scene repeatedly at his garden and its surroundings to explore how the same location could appear differently under various conditions of time and season. Among his most famous works is "Impression, Sunrise," from which the term "Impressionism" is derived to describe the movement. Other notable series include "Water Lilies," "Rouen Cathedral," and "Haystacks." Monet's paintings are still widely exhibited around the world and are considered quintessential examples of Impressionist art."

Impression, Sunrise

This painting, from which the Impressionist movement takes its name, depicts a morning sunrise at the port of Le Havre.

The Japanese Bridge

This painting is part of a series that features a wooden bridge over the lily pond in Monet's garden at Giverny.

The Cliffs at Étretat

This painting captures the majestic cliffs along the Normandy coast of France, vividly portraying the grandeur of nature and the dynamic force of the sea.

Haystacks (Sunset)

"Haystacks (Sunset)" captures the moment just as the sun sets, casting a warm glow that illuminates the haystacks with rich, golden hues.
